Understanding Floods

Introduction

Floods are natural disasters that can have devastating effects on communities. They occur when there is an overflow of water onto land that is usually dry.

This website aims to provide information and raise awareness about floods, their causes, and preventive measures.

Types of Flood:-

Flash Flood:

They occur within a few hours of heavy rainfall or abrupt changes in weather conditions. They can be highly dangerous and unpredictable, as they can occur without a warning.

River Flood:

They occur when a river overflows its banks, affecting downstream areas. River floods can last longer than flash floods but are usually more predictable and allow for better preparation.

Coastal Floods:

They occur when rising seawater floods coastal areas, causing inundation and erosion. Coastal floods are influenced by sea level rise and extreme weather events.
